Ocarinagrams

Fall 2015

Ocarinagrams was about using shape grammars to extend an earlier project, O. S. Ocarina. In O. S. Ocarina, I had found a simple way of manufacturing a small instrument, called an ocarina, from laser cut pieces of 1/8 inch thick wood. In ocarinagrams, I aimed to explore the affects of shape on sound.

A recurring theme in shape grammars is defining a set of rules in order to create a final geometry. I defined just two in order to create my design space; the space of all possible designes generable from my rules. I could either add a chamber or transform a chamber into a mouthpiece.



The first rule produces a well known design space with eight unique forms; four of which are cyclic (cannot go on forever) and four of which can.



I selected several to experiment with and construct. In order to streamline the build process, I designed a single piece that feeds into itself to construct all designs in the designs space.



The resulting find was that the futher the extremeties of the shape, the lower the sound quality. Designs that had a simple internal geometry tended to sound better, but more complex designs had more harmonics. This happens because in more complex designs, not all of the air inside the ocarina resonates in the same way, like in a regular helmholtz resonator.
