Net Zero Office

Tools used: Rhino

Fall 2015 - Collaborator: Bailey Zuniga

Net Zero Office was about sustainable building. My collaborator and I set out to use a set of energy use analysis tools in order to shape our design of an office building around being as energy conservative as possible. We were presented with a plot of land in Phoenix, AZ and made use of the advantages of natural lighting and ventilation.




In order to determine a building shape that could accomodate the number of office cubicles necessary but incur too high energy loading, we started with a base design consisting of two main buildings and iterated over. Shown above are six of the key iterations we went through, hoping that keeping the building long and thin, we would be able to take advantage of ample daylighting and less stagnant air in the interior.





Shown above is the final daylighting simulation of our chosen building shape, done over normal working hours. We were able to generate an office building that could be naturally daylit over 90% of the time it would be occupied.





We then went through several zoning iterations in order to appropriately zone our building to best accomodate thermal regulation. Shown above is approximate window placement and heatmapped energy use throughout the building.





Using PV simulations in Archsim within Rhino we were able to simulate solar panel power collection over the course of a year and thus optimize panel placement and orientation. Based on the placement of typically efficient solar cells with optimized orientation on the roof of the building, we were able to generate a design with theoretical net zero energy use.





We collated annual projected energy use and energy use intensity (EUI) as the design process progressed. As shown in the above graph, through thoughtful design, we were able to pull down annual expected energy use enough to offset it entirely with projected solar panel output.
