3D Printing Aritificial Bone

Tools used: Direct wired 3D printer, Rhino

At MIT I performed research in the Laboratory for Atomistic and Molecular Mechanics, examining 3D printing patterns and structures for hydroxyapatite based 'inks'. I assembled a direct wired 3D printer to print artificial bone, and carried out simulation processes for 3D print pattern testing. I designed and implemented a pressure based system to extured gelated hydroxyapatite 'inks' for the prince process. Part of this research lead to a publication in Journal of Biomechanical Engineering, which can be found here.
