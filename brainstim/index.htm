Galvanic vestibular stimulation

Galvanic vestibular stimulation (GVS) is an emerging technology that looks like a pair of headphones but elicits sensations of motion. It works by stimulating the vestibular system via electrodes placed on the mastoids (bones behind the ears) to generate sensations of acceleration, roll, pitch, and yaw. Here, I experiment with GVS as a means of improving visiual perception.
